Research

We are conducting a broad scope of research in the area of computing science/engineering.

In particular, we mainly focus on the following three research areas.

  • Real-Time Scheduling and Systems
  • Software Defined Batteries
  • Mobile Computing and Systems

Research Area


  • Real-Time Scheduling and Systems

    Timing guarantees for safety- and mission-critical systems





    (Click here for details.)


    This research aims at providing timing guarantees for safety- or mission-critical systems, such as real-time systems and cyber-physical systems. In particular, we develop real-time scheduling algorithms and their analysis techniques for timing guarantees. The research topics include real-time scheduling in uniprocessor and homogeneous/heterogeneous multiprocessor, mixed-criticality scheduling, security issues for real-time scheduling, real-time guarnatees for SSD, and real-time control for cyber-physical systems.


    Related Publications: RTSS2019A, RTSS 2019B, IoTJ2019A, IoTJ2019B, RTAS2019, RTSS2018, TPDS2018, RTAS2018A, RTAS2018B, RTSS2017, TC2017, TPDS2017A, TPDS2017B, RTSS2015B, TC2015, RTSS2014B, TC2014, TPDS2014A, TPDS2014B, TECS2014


  • Software Defined Batteries

    Improvement of battery performance through computer science/engineering principles





    (Click here for details.)


    In this research area, we aim at improving battery performance by controlling them with computer science/engineering principles. The research topics include battery management systems for electric vehicles battery scheduling for mobile devices, and scheduling for reconfigurable battery systems.


    Related Publications: TPDS2020, ESL2020, RTSS2019B, E-energy2019, EL2017, RTSS2016, RTSS2015A, RTSS2014A, ICCPS2014


  • Mobie Computing and Systems

    Facilitating new functions for mobile devices without additional hardware





    (Click here for details.)


    In this research area, we aims at developing new functions for mobile devices without additional hardware. The research topics include mobile applications and services using acoustic signature, and Internet of Things (IoT).


    Related Publications: PAUC2020, IEICE2019
